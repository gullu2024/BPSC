What is the Green GDP with Respect to India and Bihar?

Introduction to Green GDP

Green GDP (Green Gross Domestic Product) is an economic measure that accounts for environmental degradation, resource depletion, and ecological damage while calculating GDP. It provides a more sustainable perspective on economic growth by considering the environmental costs of economic activities.

Green GDP in India

  • Objective: To include environmental costs in economic growth measurements and promote sustainable development.
  • Implementation: India has initiated efforts to introduce Green GDP through frameworks like the Environmental Performance Index (EPI) and National Green Accounting.
  • Challenges: Lack of comprehensive data, valuation difficulties of ecosystem services, and policy execution hurdles.

Green GDP in Bihar

  • Significance: Bihar, with its reliance on agriculture and natural resources, needs Green GDP to measure economic growth while preserving ecological balance.
  • Government Initiatives: Programs like Jal-Jeevan-Hariyali, afforestation drives, and sustainable agricultural practices aim to align with Green GDP principles.
  • Challenges: Industrial pollution, deforestation, and over-exploitation of natural resources hinder effective Green GDP implementation.

Conclusion

Green GDP is a crucial metric for India and Bihar to ensure economic development does not come at the cost of environmental sustainability. Strengthening policies and adopting sustainable practices will help achieve balanced growth.
