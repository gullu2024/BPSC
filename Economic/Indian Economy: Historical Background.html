Historical Background of the Indian Economy

1. Pre-Colonial Era (Before 1757)

  • Agrarian Economy: Dominated by agriculture, handicrafts, and trade.
  • Global Trade Hub: India accounted for ~25% of global GDP in 1700.

2. Colonial Era (1757–1947)

  • Deindustrialization: Destruction of handicrafts (e.g., Bengal’s muslin industry).
  • Drain of Wealth: Exploitation of resources and heavy taxation.

3. Post-Independence (1947–1991)

  • Mixed Economy Model: Nehru-Mahalanobis strategy with Five-Year Plans.
  • Green Revolution: Boosted foodgrain production.

4. Liberalization Era (1991–Present)

  • 1991 LPG Reforms: Liberalization, privatization, globalization.
  • Rising GDP: Growth averaged 6-7% post-2000.

Economic Growth & Development: Key Phases

1. Agriculture

  • Green Revolution: Self-reliance in food production.
  • Challenges: Low productivity, farmer distress.

2. Industry

  • Post-1991 Growth: Automobiles, pharmaceuticals, telecom.
  • Current Focus: Make in India, PLI schemes.

3. Services

  • IT Revolution: Contributed 9.3% to GDP (2023).
  • Gig Economy: Startups like Flipkart, Ola.

India’s $5 Trillion Economy Goal: Critical Analysis

Government’s Strategy

  • Boost Manufacturing: PLI schemes ($26 billion incentives).
  • Infrastructure: National Infrastructure Pipeline (₹111 lakh crore by 2025).

Critical Challenges

  • Agrarian Crisis: 45% workforce contributes 15% to GDP.
  • Jobless Growth: Unemployment rate at 7.8% (2023).

UPSC-Focused Discussion Points

Theme Key Issues

Policy Debates State vs. market, inclusive growth.

       ⇅

Conclusion

  • Achieving the $5 trillion target requires addressing structural bottlenecks and leveraging demographic dividends.
  • Success hinges on institutional reforms, social sector investments, and global collaboration.
