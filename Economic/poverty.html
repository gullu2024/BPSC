1. Definition and Dimensions of Poverty

Poverty is a multifaceted phenomenon encompassing not just income deprivation but also lack of access to education, healthcare, sanitation, and social security.

  • Absolute Poverty: Survival below the minimum income threshold (World Bank: $2.15/day as of 2022).
  • Relative Poverty: Inequality within society (e.g., bottom 10% in urban India).
  • Multidimensional Poverty Index (MPI): NITI Aayog (2023) identifies deprivation in health, education, and living standards. India reduced MPI from 24.85% (2015-16) to 11.28% (2022-23).

Key Thinkers:

  • Amartya Sen: "Poverty is the deprivation of basic capabilities, not just low income."
  • Mahatma Gandhi: "Poverty is the worst form of violence."

2. Types of Poverty

  1. Situational Poverty: Temporary due to crises (e.g., COVID-19 pushed 75 million Indians into poverty in 2020, World Bank).
  2. Generational Poverty: Chronic deprivation (e.g., SC/ST communities in Bihar with 33.7% MPI).
  3. Urban Poverty: Slums lacking sanitation (Mumbai’s Dharavi).
  4. Rural Poverty: Agrarian distress (Bihar’s 76% rural population, 40% agricultural laborers).

3. Causes of Poverty

  • Structural: Unemployment (8.11% in Dec 2023, CMIE), landlessness.
  • Social: Caste (Bihar’s Musahars), gender (33% female workforce participation).
  • Policy Gaps: Poor implementation (PDS leakage at 40%, CAG 2022).
  • Regional Disparities: Bihar’s per capita income ₹54,383 (2023) vs. national ₹1.72 lakh.

4. Poverty in Bihar: A Case Study

  • Data: MPI reduced from 51.9% (2015-16) to 33.7% (2022-23), yet highest in India (NITI Aayog).
  • Challenges: Floods (18% GDP loss annually), low industrialization (9% GSVA), caste-based inequality.

Bihar CM Nitish Kumar: "Bihar’s poverty is rooted in historical neglect and agrarian stagnation."

5. Government Initiatives

National

  • NFSA, 2013: Covers 80 crore Indians; Bihar has 8.6 crore beneficiaries.
  • MGNREGA: Generated 259 crore person-days (2022-23); Bihar contributed 15%.
  • PM-KISAN: ₹6,000/year to farmers; Bihar disbursed ₹12,000 crore (2023).
  • Jal Jeevan Mission: 63% rural Bihar households have tap water (vs. 4% in 2019).

Bihar-Specific

  • JEEViKA: 1.2 crore women in SHGs, ₹15,000 crore credit mobilized (2023).
  • Student Credit Card: 5 lakh beneficiaries since 2021.

6. Global Perspectives and SDGs

  • SDG 1: 700 million globally live on <$2.15/day (World Bank, 2022).

Nelson Mandela: "Overcoming poverty is not a task of charity, it is an act of justice."

7. Challenges and Way Forward

  • Climate Resilience: Bihar’s flood management (e.g., embankments).
  • Skill Development: Align with Bihar’s Industrial Policy 2023.
  • Social Justice: Strengthen affirmative action for Mahadalits.
